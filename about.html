About F2L

Everything you need to understand the method before learning the cases.

What is CFOP?

CFOP (also called Fridrich Method) is the most popular speedcubing method. It solves the cube in four steps:

  • Cross — solve the four edge pieces on the bottom face
  • F2L — fill the four corner-edge pairs in the first two layers
  • OLL — orient all pieces on the last layer (57 cases)
  • PLL — permute all pieces on the last layer (21 cases)

F2L is where most of your time is spent and where the biggest gains come from. Learning all 41 cases eliminates the need to break the cross apart, saving 10–20 seconds per solve.

Prerequisites

Before learning F2L you should be able to:

  • Solve a Rubik's cube using any method (beginner layer-by-layer is fine)
  • Consistently solve the cross intuitively without algorithms
  • Read WCA move notation (explained below)

All cases on this site assume white cross on bottom, yellow on top — the standard CFOP orientation. All algorithms target the front-right (FR) slot.

Move Notation

Every algorithm uses standard WCA notation. Each letter represents one face of the cube. A plain letter means a 90° clockwise turn. A prime (') means counter-clockwise. A 2 means 180°.

How F2L Works

The first two layers contain four corner-edge pairs. Each pair shares two colors — for example, on a standard cube with white bottom, the front-right slot holds the white-red-green corner and the red-green edge.

The goal of F2L is to join each corner with its matching edge (form a "pair") and insert the pair into the correct slot simultaneously. Done well, this is faster than solving the corner and edge separately.

Each of the 41 cases describes a unique position and orientation of one corner-edge pair relative to its slot. The case determines which algorithm inserts it most efficiently.

Recommended Learning Order

Work through categories in this order:

  1. Easy Cases — Pair is already formed. Just slot it in. These are intuitive and build confidence. 4 cases →
  2. Both in Top — The standard F2L situation. Both pieces are free, various orientations. Learn these next. 17 cases →
  3. Corner in Top — Corner is free in the top layer; edge is already in a middle slot and needs to be paired. 8 cases →
  4. Corner in Slot — Corner is already in a slot (right or wrong place), edge needs to come from the top. 8 cases →
  5. Advanced — Pieces trapped in the wrong slots. Extraction required. Learn these last. 4 cases →

How to Use This Site

Each case card shows an animated 3D cube in the scrambled (pre-algorithm) state. Press Play to watch the algorithm execute. Press Reset to return to the case state.

When you can recognize and execute a case without looking at the algorithm, press Mark as Learned. Your progress is saved in your browser and shown in the progress bar at the top of the cases page.

Use the category filter buttons to focus on one group at a time. The URL updates so you can bookmark or share a filtered view directly.
